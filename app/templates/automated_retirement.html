Understanding Expected Returns
Key Concept: Expected Returns vs. Monthly Savings

The expected return rate is our assumption about how much your investments will grow annually. This affects how much you need to save monthly to reach your retirement goal.

How It Works:
  • Higher Returns = Lower Monthly Savings
  • Lower Returns = Higher Monthly Savings
  • This is because compound growth does more of the work
Example: $1M Target
  • 5% Returns: Save ~$1,200/month
  • 6.5% Returns: Save ~$800/month
  • 8% Returns: Save ~$600/month
Important Notes:
  • These are assumptions, not guarantees - Actual market returns may differ
  • Historical averages - Based on long-term market performance
  • Risk vs. Reward - Higher expected returns usually mean higher risk
  • Conservative approach - We use industry-standard, realistic assumptions
